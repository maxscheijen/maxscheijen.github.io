Investing Your Money without Investing Your Time

How I invest my disposable income

Don’t want to spend time thinking about investing but I also don’t want my money to become less valuable over time
investing
money

Incorporating investments into your personal finance strategy can play a significant role in building your wealth over time. With a variety of investment options to choose from, there are opportunities to suit different preferences and goals. I chose to invest into the two popular options, exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and keeping my money in a savings account because I don’t want to think about investing.

From a moral standpoint, I must admit that I am not particularly fond of using capital to generate passive gains. However, in the current climate of inflation, I utilize my disposable income generated from my labor to invest in the overall economy. Where in my opinion investing in index funds is the least harmful when compared to investing for example in real-estate.

My overall investment goals are:

  1. Keep disposable income/savings from depreciating over time (inflation).
  2. Keep savings - excisable (liquidity) - buffer for unseen situations.
  3. Minimize the time spent on investing.

Let’s look at how I try to accomplish this.

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)

ETFs are a type of investment fund that is traded on a stock exchange, much like individual stocks. ETFs are designed to track the performance of a particular index, such as the S&P 500, by investing in a portfolio of stocks that make up the index. ETFs are popular because they offer diversification, low fees, and flexibility.

I believe that no one can accurately predict the market or economy, and while some individuals may possess that ability, I personally do not have the time or desire to invest in developing that skill. Consequently, I advocate for a long-term investment approach in a diversified market fund and let time be your friend.

So why do I invest in ETFs?

  1. Diversification: By investing in an ETF, you are essentially buying a basket of stocks that represent a particular index, sector or market. This diversification can help reduce the risk and volatility in your investment portfolio. For example, instead of investing in a single stock, you can invest in an ETF that tracks the performance of an entire index or sector. I mostly buy funds that provide me with exposure to the global equity market such as Vanguard FTSE All-World UCITS ETF.

  2. Low fees: ETFs typically have lower fees than mutual funds because they are passively managed. This means that they track the performance of a particular index or sector, rather than actively trying to beat the market. This passive management approach results in lower fees, which can help you keep more of your returns.

  3. Flexible: ETFs can be bought and sold on stock exchanges throughout the trading day, much like individual stocks. Making them accessible to a broader audience.

I use DeGiro and Brand New Day for buying ETF’s. Both brokers have low fees.

Savings Account

On the other hand, a savings account is a simple and safe option for storing money. A savings account is a deposit account held at a bank or financial institution that typically earns interest on the account balance.

Bunq Easy Savings account offers a saving interest of 2.01%, a lot higher than other banks in the Netherlands.

While savings accounts do not offer high returns, they are a low-risk option for storing money. However with central banks increasing interest rates, the interest you earn on your savings will most likely increase in the near future.

  1. Safety: Savings accounts in the Netherlands are insured by the Dutch National Bank (DNB) up to €100,000 per account. This means that if the bank or financial institution fails, the account holder is protected up to the insurance limit.

  2. liquidity: Unlike some other investment options, such as stocks or real estate, savings accounts are highly liquid. This means that the money can be easily accessed when needed, without penalties or fees for withdrawal.

Conclusion

In conclusion, investing in ETFs and keeping money in a savings account are two popular options for investing money. ETFs offer diversification, low fees, and flexibility, while savings accounts offer safety and liquidity. When deciding where to invest money, it is important to consider individual goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.
